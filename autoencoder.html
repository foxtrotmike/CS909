MNIST autoencoder with interactive latent space

This browser demo trains a deep autoencoder in TensorFlow.js, projects MNIST digits into a 2D latent space, and lets you click anywhere in that space to decode a generated digit. A clicked point does not need to come from the training set.

(c) Fayyaz Minhas.

How to use it

After training, click inside the latent plot. The decoder will generate a digit from that 2D location. The demo also shows the nearest embedded test digit so you can compare the generated image to nearby data.

Notes

This is a vanilla autoencoder, not a VAE. So the latent space is learned only through reconstruction. That means some regions may decode into blurrier or less digit-like outputs, especially far from the training manifold.

To keep the demo responsive in the browser, it trains on a subset of MNIST rather than the full dataset by default.
